University Projects

1st Year Project - Feral

Feral is a 2D action-platformer developed as part of a collaborative team project in Unity using C#, with version control through GitHub. The game follows a hybrid subject escaping a deteriorating research facility, combining platforming, combat mechanics and environmental storytelling within a sci-fi setting. Within the team, I took a lead role in narrative design and dialogue writing, helping shape the tone, characters and world-building. I was also lead in character design and art and environment art. In addition to creative work, I supported aspects of scripting and level design, ensuring narrative elements were effectively integrated into gameplay and progression. Working on Feral allowed me to develop key collaborative skills, including using GitHub, coordinating tasks and adapting to shared deadlines. It also highlighted the importance of communication and consistency as multiple disciplines must align. Overall, the project strengthened both my technical and interpersonal skills, giving me valuable experience in developing a cohesive game as part of a structured team.

1st Year Project - Flicker

Flicker is a 2D atmospheric narrative demo developed in Unity using C#, created as my first independent game project where I completed all programming, art and animation. The game follows a small rat navigating a decaying world with a firefly, blending a walking-simulator style experience with quest systems and environmental storytelling. It was designed to explore my 2D development capabilities, focusing on creating mood through desaturated pixel art, dynamic lighting and character dialogue. I implemented core mechanics such as movement, item-based progression and NPC quest systems. Flicker also allowed me to explore more complex conceptual design, including a fully realised ecosystem and lore system centred around the “Heartglow,” reinforcing themes of decay, connection and hope. Overall, the project helped me understand the scope of what I could achieve independently.

1st Year Project - FrostBound

Frostbound is a first-person 3D demo developed in Unity, created as my first exploration into 3D level design and storytelling using ProBuilder. I constructed a cohesive snowy environment including log cabins, interior spaces, distant structures and natural elements such as rivers, terrain and foliage, while keeping reliance on external assets minimal. The experience centres on a reclusive character who hates Christmas, with his personality and backstory conveyed through environmental contrast and dialogue. Subtle design choices such as identical decorated houses lining the road versus the player’s dim, undecorated home, decaying trees and flickering lighting are used to direct progression and reinforce narrative tone. Simple mechanics support a walking-simulator style experience where atmosphere takes priority over gameplay complexity. I also experimented with particle systems, lighting, post-processing effects and texture to create a cold, melancholic mood. Overall, Frostbound was a successful first look at 3D development independently.

1st Year Project - The Eternal Circus

The Eternal Circus is a physical card game developed as part of a game design theory and iteration study, with a focus on core mechanics, accessibility and playtesting-driven development. I designed it to be quick to learn and suitable for a wide range of ages and experience levels, the game centres around players collecting character-specific prop cards while using action cards to disrupt opponents, creating a balance of strategy, chance and social interaction. Throughout development, I independently designed all visual elements, including hand-drawn card artwork created in Ibis Paint X, as well as the card backs, rulebook and packaging using Canva. A major focus of the project was iterative design; the game was continuously playtested and refined to improve clarity, pacing and player engagement, ensuring the rules remained simple. This project strengthened my understanding of fundamental game design principles, particularly with mechanics, themes and player experience.
