1st Year Project - Flicker

Flicker is a 2D atmospheric narrative demo developed in Unity using C#, created as my first independent game project where I completed all programming, art and animation. The game follows a small rat navigating a decaying world with a firefly, blending a walking-simulator style experience with quest systems and environmental storytelling. It was designed to explore my 2D development capabilities, focusing on creating mood through desaturated pixel art, dynamic lighting and character dialogue. I implemented core mechanics such as movement, item-based progression and NPC quest systems. Flicker also allowed me to explore more complex conceptual design, including a fully realised ecosystem and lore system centred around the “Heartglow,” reinforcing themes of decay, connection and hope. Overall, the project helped me understand the scope of what I could achieve independently while pushing into more ambitious narrative and aesthetic design.

1st Year Project - FrostBound

Frostbound is a first-person 3D environmental narrative demo developed in Unity, created as an exploration into 3D level design and spatial storytelling using ProBuilder. As my first project working fully in 3D, I focused on constructing a cohesive snowy environment from the ground up, including log cabins, interior spaces, distant structures and natural elements such as rivers, terrain and foliage, while keeping reliance on external assets minimal. The experience centres on a reclusive character who openly rejects the festive atmosphere surrounding him, with his personality and backstory conveyed entirely through environmental contrast, sparse dialogue and player-guided discovery. Subtle design choices such as identical decorated houses lining the road versus the player’s dim, undecorated home, decaying trees and flickering lighting are used to direct progression and reinforce narrative tone. Simple mechanics, including movement, interaction prompts and invisible colliders for soft guidance, support a walking-simulator style experience where atmosphere takes priority over gameplay complexity. I also experimented with particle systems, lighting, post-processing effects and texture work to create a cold, melancholic mood. Overall, Frostbound represents a successful shift into 3D development, demonstrating my ability to build immersive environments and communicate narrative through space, tone and visual design alone.

1st Year Project - The Eternal Circus

The Eternal Circus is a physical card game developed as part of a game design theory and iteration study, with a focus on core mechanics, accessibility and playtesting-driven development. I designed it to be quick to learn and suitable for a wide range of ages and experience levels, the game centres around players collecting character-specific prop cards while using action cards to disrupt opponents, creating a balance of strategy, chance and social interaction. Throughout development, I independently designed all visual elements, including hand-drawn card artwork created in Ibis Paint X, as well as the card backs, rulebook and packaging using Canva. A major focus of the project was iterative design; the game was continuously playtested and refined to improve clarity, pacing and player engagement, ensuring the rules remained simple without sacrificing depth. Thematically, the game explores a darker narrative of performers trapped in an endless circus, with mechanics reinforcing tension and competition between players. This project strengthened my understanding of fundamental game design principles, particularly how mechanics, theme and player experience can align effectively without reliance on digital systems.
